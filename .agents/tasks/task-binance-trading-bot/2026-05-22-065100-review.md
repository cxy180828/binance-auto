## High-level view

Position persistence uses a `positions` table with `INSERT OR REPLACE` keyed on symbol. Positions are saved once at buy time and deleted at sell time, but the `manage_positions` loop mutates `highest_price`, `trailing_stop_active`, and `trailing_stop_price` every tick without flushing those updates. On crash, restored positions will have stale stop state â€” the trailing stop price could be lower than what it reached in-memory.

The credential story is partially fixed: env vars take priority and `config.yaml.example` exists, but `config.yaml` itself is already committed and remains tracked. Running `git rm --cached config.yaml` would complete the fix.

The blacklist seeding catches all exceptions and logs a warning. The failure mode is fail-open: a corrupt database or missing table results in an empty blacklist, and symbols that should be suppressed trade freely. For a loss-prevention mechanism, fail-open is the wrong default.

## Position persistence gap during manage_positions

`manage_positions` modifies position state every tick â€” `highest_price` ratchets up, `trailing_stop_active` flips from False to True, `trailing_stop_price` increases via the `max()` guard â€” without calling `save_position`. If the process crashes after the trailing stop activates at a high water mark but before the sell triggers, the restored position will have `trailing_stop_active=False` and `highest_price` at entry price (the values from buy time). The position will be managed as if it just entered, potentially holding through a decline that should have triggered the stop.

The fix is a single `self.storage.save_position(position)` call at the end of the position loop in `manage_positions`, after all mutations are applied. The SQLite write per tick per position is cheap for 10 positions.
